Hidden Gems, High Peaks, & Lucerne's Best Kept Secrets

Michael Boroda
May 27, 2024

More and more backpackers are flocking to Lucerne, Switzerland. 

This is because it’s one of the most gorgeous places to visit on the planet, with incredible alpine views, a stunning lake, and a gorgeous old-town - AKA Altstadt. 

You may have read our first blog post about Lucerne, and the budget friendly travel activities you can partake in while you’re there. And we haven’t covered them all - not even close. 

So without further ado, here are three more incredibly life-altering, backpacking experiences you can have in Lucerne, Switzerland, while staying within your budget. 

TABLE OF CONTENTS: 

  1. Lucerne's Frozen Past: From Glacial Grooves to Heroic Lions

  2. Wheels and Stars: The Swiss Museum of Transport & Planetarium Experience

  3. Melodies in the Mountains: Enjoy Free Music Under the Swiss Sky

1. Lucerne's Frozen Past: From Glacial Grooves to Heroic Lions

One of the coolest things you’ll get a chance to do in Lucerne is to check out the Glacier Garden and its accompanying Lion Monument. These two sites, located conveniently close to each other, provide a glimpse into the city's geological and cultural heritage.

Usually, the experience begins at Glacier Garden, which is a natural monument that dates all the way back to the Ice Age - even though it was only discovered in 1872. 

This site is home to stunning glacial potholes formed during the Ice Age - well the last ice age - which was around 20,000 years ago. As you walk through the garden, you’ll see these deep, smooth depressions in the rock, created by the grinding force of retreating glaciers.

The Glacier Garden also features a museum where you can learn about the region’s geological history and the evolution of its landscape. The exhibits include fossils, relief maps, and displays that illustrate how glaciers shaped the Swiss Alps. 

Also, don’t miss the mirror maze, which is almost like a funhouse hall of mirrors. 

Meanwhile, The Lion Monument (Löwendenkmal) - which is just a short walk from the Glacier Garden - is one of Lucerne’s most poignant and visited landmarks. 

Carved directly into a sandstone rock face, this  sculpture commemorates the Swiss Guards who were massacred during the French Revolution in 1792.

The lion, depicted mortally wounded and lying in a peaceful grotto, represents the bravery and sacrifice of the soldiers. The scale, detailed craftsmanship, and the emotional depth of the monument and what it represents to the Swiss make it a must-see for visitors to Lucerne. 

Mark Twain famously described the monument as "the most mournful and moving piece of stone in the world."

The Glacier Garden and the Lion Monument offer a unique blend of natural history and human emotion. They stand as testaments to the powerful forces – both geological and historical – that have shaped Lucerne. 

Visiting these sites not only enriches your understanding of the city; also provides a deeper appreciation of the enduring legacies of nature and heroism.

Some Practical Tips for Visiting

  • Timing: Both attractions are open year-round, and check their websites for specific opening hours and any seasonal changes.

  • Tickets: While the lion monument is free to visit, the Glacier Garden is €22, or €17 if you’re a student. You can receive a free admission if you possess a Swiss Museum Pass, a Lucerne Museum Pass, or any of the other passes listed under Free Admission in this link. 

  • Accessibility: The Glacier Garden has several steps and uneven paths, so wear comfortable shoes. The Lion Monument is easily accessible and can be viewed from a park setting.

2. Wheels and Stars: The Swiss Museum of Transport & Planetarium Experience 

Up next we have one of the most enriching yet affordable experiences you can have in Lucerne: The Swiss Museum of Transport. 

With its fascinating planetarium - the largest in the country -, as well as exhibits depicting the history of trains, planes, and automobiles, it’s a must-visit destination for anyone while you’re here. 

The museum offers a comprehensive look at Switzerland’s transport history, and the planetarium provides a captivating journey through the cosmos. Together, they make for a day packed with discovery and wonder.

The Swiss Museum of Transport (Verkehrshaus der Schweiz) is the most visited museum in Switzerland, and for good reason. Spanning various modes of transportation, this museum showcases the evolution of travel and communication in Switzerland. Here's what you can expect:

  1. Land Transport:

    • Trains and Railways: Dive into the history of Swiss railways, featuring an impressive collection of locomotives, carriages, and railway artifacts. Interactive exhibits let you simulate driving a train or even planning a rail network.

    • Automobiles: The car exhibition includes vintage models, sports cars, and a deep dive into the history of automobile manufacturing and design in Switzerland.

  2. Air and Space:

    • Aviation History: Explore the development of aviation from early biplanes to modern jets. There are several aircraft on display, including a full-scale model of the Solar Impulse, a solar-powered aircraft.

    • Space Exploration: Discover Switzerland's contributions to space travel, with models and information about satellites and space missions.

  3. Water Transport:

    • Maritime Exhibits: The museum houses a variety of boats, from historical steamboats to modern yachts. Learn about the significance of Switzerland’s lakes and rivers in transport history.

  4. Communication:

    • Media Evolution: The communication section of the museum covers the history of media and telecommunication, showcasing old radios, televisions, and telephones. Interactive displays allow you to understand how communication technologies have transformed over the years.

Meanwhile, the planetarium - located within the Swiss Museum of Transport - offers a breathtaking escape from the terrestrial exhibits. It's one of the most advanced planetariums in Europe, featuring state-of-the-art technology that brings the universe to life:

  • Astronomical Shows: The planetarium hosts regular shows that take you on a journey through the solar system, the Milky Way, and beyond. These shows are narrated in multiple languages, including English, ensuring accessibility for international visitors.

  • Interactive Experiences: Some shows are interactive, allowing you to explore constellations, planets, and deep-space phenomena. It's an educational experience that’s as entertaining as it is informative.

  • Special Events: The planetarium often holds special events, such as guest lectures by astronomers or themed nights focusing on particular celestial events.

Both the museum and the planetarium offer educational experiences that are engaging for all ages. They provide a deep dive into technological advancements and the natural world.

They also offer tons of hands-on / interactive exhibits to make learning fun and memorable, even for adults. Whether you’re piloting a virtual plane or exploring the night sky, these interactive elements make the visit that much more fun.

Tickets are also super reasonably priced considering the value that you get - costing between €12.50 - €50 depending on what sort of pass you purchase -, so you don’t have to worry about that if you’re on a budget - like I was when I visited. The value you receive from a full day of exploration is well worth it. Plus, there are often discounts for students and backpackers.

Practical Tips for Your Visit

  • Timing: Plan to spend at least half a day here. The museum is extensive, and you’ll want to catch a show at the planetarium.

  • Tickets: Purchase your tickets online in advance to avoid queues. Combo tickets for the museum and planetarium are available and can save you money.

  • Location: The museum is conveniently located and easily accessible by public transport. It’s a short bus ride from the Lucerne city center.

  • Amenities: The museum has a café and picnic areas, so you can take a break and enjoy a meal without leaving the premises.

A visit to the Swiss Museum of Transport and its planetarium is a highlight of any trip to Lucerne. It offers a unique blend of history, technology, and celestial wonder that appeals to curious minds and adventurers alike. Whether you’re a transport enthusiast or a star gazer, this is one stop you won’t want to miss. Dive into the worlds of wheels and stars and discover the stories that drive Switzerland forward.

3. Melodies in the Mountains: Enjoy Free Music Under the Swiss Sky

I’ll never forget walking back to my hostel at around 6:00 p.m. one, sunny evening during my visit to Lucerne in late May / early June of 2019. 

I was dealing with a bit of a cold, so I wanted to get some dinner from a grocery store - I think I got sushi - and just chill for a little bit. 

On my way back to the hostel, however, I ran into a family standing outside the Lucerne Cultural & Congress Centre - basically the Lucerne concert hall - trying to find someone to go to the opera with as they had a free / extra ticket available. 

The father asked me, “Do you like opera?” I nodded. He showed me the ticket and said I could have it for free. 

How on earth was I ever going to say, “No”, to that - even if I was feeling a bit under the weather? 

I nodded and took him up on it. It wound up being one of the best, and most memorable experiences I had on my travels.  

Even though the opera took place indoors, it was free - as I think they were members of the center -, and Lucerne offers a large and incredible tapestry of cultural experiences, some of which are gratis; others of which are just plain old fun and affordable. 

Among these are some of the city’s free outdoor concerts and events that stand out to provide a unique blend of natural beauty and musical enjoyment. This guide will delve into how to make the most of Lucerne’s vibrant music scene without spending a dime.

Discovering Lucerne’s Outdoor Music Scene Lucerne boasts a lively music scene that takes full advantage of its picturesque settings. From classical music to modern pop and jazz, there’s something for every musical taste. Here are some highlights and tips on where to find these enchanting outdoor performances:

  1. Lucerne Festival in Summer:

    • What to Expect: The Lucerne Festival is one of the most prestigious music festivals in Europe, attracting world-class musicians and conductors. While many events require tickets, the festival also features free outdoor concerts.

    • Where to Go: Concerts are often held in public spaces such as the lakeside promenade or city squares, where locals and tourists alike can enjoy the music in a relaxed atmosphere.

    • Highlights: Don’t miss the "In the Streets" series, which brings music to various parts of the city, turning Lucerne’s streets into a vibrant concert hall.

  2. Sunset Music Sessions:

    • What to Expect: These are informal gatherings where local musicians perform as the sun sets over Lake Lucerne, creating a really chill ambiance.

    • Where to Go: Look for performances along the lakefront, especially near Seebad Luzern, a popular lakeside swimming and lounging area.

    • Highlights: Bring a blanket and some snacks, and enjoy the mix of acoustic sets, jazz, and folk music while watching the sun dip behind the mountains.

  3. Concerts in the Parks:

    • What to Expect: During the warmer months, Lucerne’s parks become venues for free concerts featuring a variety of genres, from classical orchestras to contemporary bands.

    • Where to Go: Ufschötti Park and Inseli Park are popular spots for these events. They offer ample space for lounging and enjoying music in a relaxed, family-friendly environment.

    • Highlights: These concerts often coincide with local festivals, offering a great way to experience Swiss culture and community spirit.

Making the Most of Free Music Events To fully enjoy Lucerne’s free music offerings, a bit of planning and local knowledge can enhance your experience:

  • Check Local Listings: Websites like Lucerne's official tourism page, local event calendars, and community boards are excellent resources for up-to-date information on free concerts and events.

  • Arrive Early: Free events can attract large crowds, especially during peak tourist season. Arriving early ensures you get a good spot and can settle in comfortably.

  • Pack a Picnic: Enhance your experience by bringing along some local snacks or a full picnic. Many locals do the same, creating a festive, communal atmosphere.

  • Dress Comfortably: Outdoor events can mean sitting on grass or park benches for extended periods. Dress in layers to stay comfortable as temperatures can drop in the evening.

Why These Events Are a Must-Visit

  1. Cultural Immersion: Free outdoor concerts provide a genuine taste of local culture. You’ll hear Swiss music styles you might not encounter elsewhere and experience how locals enjoy their city.

  2. Scenic Backdrops: Few places can match the scenic beauty of Lucerne as a backdrop for musical performances. The combination of stunning landscapes and melodic tunes creates a memorable experience.

  3. Community Vibes: These events are a great way to meet both locals and fellow travelers. The communal enjoyment of music fosters a welcoming and friendly atmosphere.

Ultimately, Lucerne’s free outdoor concerts and events are a testament to the city’s rich cultural fabric and natural beauty. Whether you’re a music aficionado or simply looking to enjoy a relaxing evening under the Swiss sky, these events offer unforgettable experiences without straining your budget. So, tune into the melodies of the mountains and let Lucerne’s musical charm wash over you.
